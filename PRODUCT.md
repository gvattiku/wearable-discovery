# Product Direction

Read this file before making changes.

## Vision

Help people recognize burnout patterns before gradual overload starts to feel normal.

Amiburnt is a product discovery project, not a finished wellness product. The goal is to understand whether technology can help people notice subtle changes in energy, routines, motivation, sleep, focus, and rest earlier, without turning self-reflection into another dashboard to manage.

## Problem

Burnout rarely happens all at once. It tends to develop slowly through small shifts that are easy to explain away in the moment:

- Lower energy
- Broken routines
- Reduced motivation
- Poorer sleep
- Duller focus
- Rest that stops feeling restorative

Existing tools often measure isolated metrics, but they do not always help people understand the bigger pattern. By the time someone names the experience as burnout, they may already be past the point where small changes would have helped.

## Hypothesis

If people can notice subtle changes across their habits and lived experience over several weeks, they may recognize gradual overload earlier and make sense of burnout patterns before those patterns feel normal.

## Target User

The initial research audience is people who have experienced gradual overload, especially knowledge workers, students, caregivers, founders, clinicians, and other people whose work or life demands make it easy to normalize exhaustion.

They may not be looking for a tracker. They may be looking for language, reflection, and a calmer way to understand what has been changing.

## Research Questions

- What changes do people notice before they describe themselves as burned out?
- Which changes are easiest to miss in the moment?
- What language feels accurate without feeling clinical, alarmist, or judgmental?
- Would people trust a product that helps them notice patterns without giving scores?
- What kind of reflection feels useful rather than burdensome?
- Where is the line between helpful awareness and another thing to manage?

## Current Assumptions

- The product should lead with the problem, not the solution.
- The experience should feel calm, human, and low-pressure.
- The story should focus on burnout patterns, gradual overload, energy drift, routine changes, and subtle shifts.
- The project should invite people to share lived experience and shape the research.
- The product should avoid positioning around stress tracking, recovery scoring, optimization, performance, biohacking, diagnosis, or clinical claims.
- The homepage should make it clear this is an early discovery effort, not a completed product pretending to be ready.

## Things We Still Don’t Know

- Whether people recognize the phrase "burnout patterns" as meaningful.
- Which early changes are most predictive, personally salient, or actionable.
- Whether people want a wearable, an app, a reflection tool, or something else entirely.
- Whether people would share sensitive burnout-related information with a product.
- What level of interpretation feels useful without becoming prescriptive.
- Whether the strongest audience is people currently near overload, people recovering from burnout, or people trying to avoid repeating it.
